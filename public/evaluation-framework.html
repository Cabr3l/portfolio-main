Introduction

Hackathons are intensive, time-constrained events where participants collaborate to solve complex problems through innovative solutions. Effective assessment requires a structured, fair, and comprehensive evaluation framework.

Time Constraints

  • • Limited development time (24-48 hours)
  • • Rapid prototyping requirements
  • • Minimal testing opportunities

Participant Diversity

  • • Varied skill levels and backgrounds
  • • Different problem-solving approaches
  • • Mixed technical expertise

Evaluation Complexity

  • • Subjective innovation assessment
  • • Balancing creativity with practicality
  • • Fair comparison across projects

Detailed Evaluation Framework

Comprehensive scoring criteria for each dimension

Innovation & Creativity (30 points)

The heart of any hackathon solution

Originality (10 points)

Completely novel approach 9-10
Significant modification 7-8
Minor improvements 5-6

Creativity (10 points)

Highly innovative use 9-10
Creative combination 7-8
Some creative elements 5-6

Disruption Potential (10 points)

Fundamental change 9-10
Significant improvement 7-8
Moderate improvement 5-6

Technical Implementation (25 points)

Quality and robustness of the technical solution

Code Quality (8 points)

Clean, readable, documented 7-8
Generally good code 5-6
Functional but poor structure 3-4

Architecture (8 points)

Well-designed, scalable 7-8
Good structure 5-6
Basic structure 3-4

Functionality (9 points)

Fully functional 8-9
Mostly functional 6-7
Partially functional 4-5

Judging Process

Structured evaluation phases for fair assessment

1

Initial Screening (Mentors)

Eliminate non-functional solutions and perform basic quality checks

Time per project: 30 minutes
Focus: Functionality & Basic Quality
2

Expert Panel Review

Technical specialists and environmental experts evaluation

Time per project: 45 minutes
Focus: Technical Depth & Environmental Impact
3

Peer Evaluation

Participants vote on other projects with anonymous feedback

Time per project: 20 minutes
Focus: Peer Perspective & Innovation
4

Final Presentation

5-minute pitch with 3-minute Q&A and live demonstration

Time per project: 8 minutes
Focus: Communication & Demo
5

Combined Scoring

Weighted average calculation and final ranking determination

Process: Weighted Average & Ranking
Outcome: Winner Announcement
